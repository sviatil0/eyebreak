EyeBreak

Gentle 20-20-20 eye breaks for your Mac. Free and open source.

May reduce eye strain and dry-eye symptoms by supporting healthier screen habits.

Install — about a minute

  1. Press ⌘ space, type Terminal, press return.
  2. Click Copy, paste into the Terminal window, press return.
  3. Follow any prompts. When it finishes, look for the eye icon in your menu bar (top right).
curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/sviatil0/eyebreak/main/scripts/install.sh | bash

The command installs Homebrew first if you don't have it — that step may ask for your Mac password.

Prefer a normal download?
  1. Download the app: latest release → the EyeBreak-…zip file.
  2. Unzip it and drag EyeBreak into your Applications folder, then double-click it.
  3. macOS will say it "cannot check it for malicious software" — that's because this free app isn't registered with Apple's paid program. Click OK.
  4. Open System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, click Open Anyway, and confirm. It opens normally from then on.

What it does

  • Every 20 minutes, a gentle reminder to look ~20 feet away for 20 seconds
  • Snooze or skip any break — it's a habit, not a rule
  • Pauses by itself during presentations and full-screen apps
  • No camera, no account, no internet connection — everything stays on your Mac

EyeBreak supports comfortable screen habits. It does not diagnose eye disease, replace an eye exam, or improve vision. See an eye-care professional for eye pain, light sensitivity, discharge, sudden vision changes, or symptoms that persist.
